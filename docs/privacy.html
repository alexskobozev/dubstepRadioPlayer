Privacy Policy — DUBSTEP.FM

Effective date: 10 June 2026 · Last updated: 10 June 2026

This policy explains what information the DUBSTEP.FM mobile app (the "app") collects, why, and how to contact the developer. The app is a thin client for the publicly accessible dubstep.fm internet radio stream and is published by Alexander Kobozev (the "developer"). The app is not affiliated with or operated by the dubstep.fm broadcaster.

What the app does not collect

  • No account, login, or sign-up — the app has none.
  • No name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, location, microphone, calendar, or health data.
  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA). The app does not show ads.
  • No tracking, in Apple's App Tracking Transparency sense — no data is linked to identity across other apps or websites.

What the app does collect

The app uses two Google Firebase services as data processors:

Firebase Crashlytics

  • What: Crash reports and non-fatal error reports — stack traces, device model, OS version, app version, and a randomly-generated installation ID.
  • Why: To detect and fix bugs that cause the app to crash or fail to play the stream.
  • Retention: Crash reports are retained for 90 days by Firebase and then automatically deleted.

Firebase Analytics

  • What: A small set of app-interaction events:
    • playback_start — when the listener taps PLAY or switches bitrate. Includes the chosen bitrate (24/64/128/256 kbps) and stream URL.
    • playback_error — when the stream fails to load or drops mid-play. Includes bitrate, stream URL, a short error description, and whether the app is going to retry.
    • playback_failed — when the app gives up after exhausted retries and shows "Can't connect". Includes bitrate, stream URL, and short error description.
  • Plus: Firebase Analytics automatically records app open, session length, app version, OS version, device model, country (derived from IP at request time — IP itself is not stored), and a randomly-generated installation ID.
  • Why: To understand which bitrates listeners use, how often the stream fails, and whether app updates regress these numbers.
  • Retention: Aggregated analytics data is retained for 14 months and then automatically deleted.

Audio stream

The app plays an audio stream served directly from the dubstep.fm broadcaster's servers (currently 50.118.246.51:80 over plain HTTP). The developer does not host, record, store, or relay the audio. What you hear is what dubstep.fm is currently broadcasting. The developer has no insight into who is listening.

Sharing & third parties

The diagnostic and analytics data described above is processed by Google as a sub-processor under the Firebase Data Processing and Security Terms. The developer does not sell, rent, share, or otherwise transfer any data to any other third party.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them. The app contains no in-app purchases, no chat, no user-generated content, and no advertising. The audio stream itself may contain language unsuitable for young children, which is why the app is rated 12+.

Your choices

  • Reset diagnostic/analytics identifiers: uninstall and reinstall the app. The installation ID is regenerated on a fresh install.
  • Stop collection entirely: uninstall the app.
  • Request deletion: email the address below with your installation ID (visible in the app's About screen — to be added in a future update) and the developer will request its deletion from Firebase.

Changes to this policy

Material changes will be reflected by bumping the "Effective date" above and, where the change is significant, noted in the app's next "What's New" release notes.
